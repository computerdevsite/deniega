Introduction

In today's digital age, online threats like viruses, malware, and spam are more common than ever. In this blog, we'll explore these threats and how antivirus software can help protect you from them.

What are Viruses?

A computer virus is a type of malicious software that, when executed, replicates by inserting copies of itself into other computer programs, data files, or the boot sector of the hard drive. Viruses can be spread via email attachments, websites, or infected software.

Understanding Malware

Malware is a broad category of harmful software that includes viruses, worms, trojans, and ransomware. These malicious programs are designed to harm your computer, steal your personal information, or even lock you out of your system.

What is Spam?

Spam refers to unwanted or unsolicited digital communications, typically emails, that are sent in bulk for malicious purposes such as spreading viruses or selling products. It's essential to recognize and avoid these threats to protect your inbox and privacy.

How Antivirus Software Helps

Antivirus software is designed to detect and eliminate malicious programs like viruses and malware from your computer. It scans files and activities on your system and can prevent new infections by blocking harmful content.
